## What the registry is for

The Parly App needs to know which relayer execution addresses are discoverable and which relayer public keys it may encrypt bundles to. That is what the registry does.

## Registry policy

The Parly registry supports a maximum of 10 live relayers.

Registration is automatic if validation passes and a slot is available. Inactive relayers are removed from live discovery so slots can reopen while keeping the registry lean and operator-grade.

## Register a relayer

Go to:

`parly.fi/relayer`

You will need:

- the relayer execution address
- the Base64 relayer public key
- a wallet signature proving control of the submitted execution address

Registration succeeds automatically if:

- the registry is open
- the address is valid
- the public key is valid
- the signature is valid
- the relayer is not a duplicate entry

## Registry states

**Verified**\
Live in app discovery.

**Inactive**\
Previously live, now removed from discovery because of inactivity, operator withdrawal, manual disable action, or failed health expectations.

**Rejected**\
Failed automatic validation.

## Important note

Listing improves public app discovery only. It does not control whether the protocol is open, and it does not prevent advanced users, SDK users, or custom frontend operators from targeting independent relayers directly.
