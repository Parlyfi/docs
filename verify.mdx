## What Verify does

Verify is the public selective-disclosure surface for Parly. It answers one narrow question well:

Does this disclosed child key correspond to a real payout lane in the referenced Parly execution?

That keeps proof useful for real operations without exposing the wider private execution set.

## What you need

To use Verify, you need:

- the confirmed Tempo transaction hash
- the disclosed child key

The payer usually shares these values in a scoped receipt or through a direct proof handoff.

## What Verify can return

Depending on the available indexed and scoped data, Verify may display:

- settlement confirmation
- payout recipient
- payout amount
- destination chain or route
- provenance details where truthfully available

The purpose is not to reveal everything. The purpose is to prove the disclosed lane cleanly and independently.

## What Verify does not do

Verify does not:

- reveal the full private execution set
- expose unrelated payout lanes
- reconstruct treasury behavior
- assign shared route economics to one disclosed lane
- replace a full internal audit system

Selective verification only works if the proof surface stays selective.

## Typical verification flow

1. Receive the confirmed Tempo transaction hash.
2. Receive the disclosed child key.
3. Open Verify.
4. Enter both values.
5. Review the matched payout result.

If the values match a real disclosed lane, Verify confirms it. If they do not, the result should fail clearly.

## Who Verify is for

Verify is for:

- counterparties verifying a payment
- exchanges checking a claimed receipt
- auditors reviewing one specific lane
- finance teams reviewing one disclosed payout
- compliance teams checking one scoped record
